Neapolitan is the original pizza. This delicious pie dates all the way back to 18th century in Naples, Italy.

Chicago pizza

Chicago pizza, also commonly referred to as deep-dish pizza, gets its name from the city it was invented in, by Ike Sewell.

Toppings are ground beef, sausage, pepperoni, onion, mushrooms, green peppers, tomato sauce with sprinkle of Parmesan cheese.

New york style pizza

With its characteristic large, foldable slices and crispy outer crust, New York-style pizza is one of America’s most famous regional pizza types

Toppings: tomato sauce and mozzarella cheese, pepperoni, sausage, mushroom, anchovies. oregano, red pepper flakes, Parmesan cheese, and garlic powder.

select

Sicilian pizza

Sicilian pizza, also known as "sfincione," is a thick cut of pizza with pillowy dough, a crunchy crust, and loads of tomato sauce.

Sicilian pizzas are often topped with bits of tomato, onion, anchovies, and herbs.

Gourmet pizza

This pizza got its start back in the late 1970’s when Chef Ed LaDou began experimenting with pizza recipes in the classic Italian restaurant, Prego.

This pizza has no as traditional toppings. This lack of specificity allows you to get inventive. You can ask for anything from chicken and artichokes to goat cheese and egg.

Greek pizza

Greek pizza was created by Greek immigrants features a medium thick and chewy crust cooked in shallow, oiled pans, resulting in a nearly deep-fried bottom.

Toppings Sauce is heavier than the cheese with a tangy tomato paste, strong oregano flavor, cheese mix, of mozzarella, cheddar or provolone, feta cheese, black olives, & red onion.

St-Louis pizza

St. Louis pizza features a thin crust with a cracker-like consistency that is made without yeast. Due to the crispy crust, it is usually cut into three- or four-inch rectangles, known as "party" or "tavern" cut.

Toppings: Provel cheese, a sweeter tomato sauce, oregano. Because of its firm crust, it can support several toppings of your choice.
