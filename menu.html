Neapolitan pizza

Neapolitan is the original pizza. This delicious pie dates all the way back to 18th century in Naples, Italy.

Toppings: tomatoes, garlic, oregano, basil, and mozzarella.

Chicago pizza

Chicago pizza, also known as to as deep-dish pizza, gets its name from the city it was invented in, by Ike Sewell.

Toppings: ground beef, sausage, pepperoni, onion, mushrooms, green peppers and cheese.

New york style pizza

With its characteristic large, foldable slices and crispy outer crust, New York-style pizza is one of America’s most famous regional pizza types.

Toppings: cheese, pepperoni, sausage, mushroom, anchovies, oregano, pepper flakes, garlic.

Sicilian pizza

Sicilian pizza, also known as "sfincione," is a thick cut of pizza with pillowy dough, a crunchy crust, and loads of tomato sauce.

Toppings: great with cheese on no cheese, onion, anchovies, and herbs.

Greek pizza

Greek pizza created by Greeks features a medium thick and chewy crust cooked in shallow, oiled pans, results in a nearly deep-fried bottom.

Toppings: oregano flavor, cheese mix of cheddar or provolone, black olives, red onion.
